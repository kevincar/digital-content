# Introduction

## A Testimony or a Chasm
The prophet Moroni at the end of the Book of Mormon posits that anyone, [if asking in faith and with real intent](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p4-p5#p4), can have the truth of the book be made known unto them by the power of the Holy Ghost. For many members and friends of the church, this realization of what Moroni challenges us to do becomes a significant initial mile stone on their covenant path. For me, I never directly had that experience—though I do have a strong witness of the reality of Jesus Christ and HisGospel—for a while, the lack of that experience, for me, began to make the difference between belief and knowing a gaping chasm as wide and as deep as the Pacific Ocean. Today, I want to talk about my personal experience with finding my way of gaining a personal witness of Jesus Christ and His Gospel, but first I want to go back to the topic of the Pacific Ocean to illustrate a secular story that I feel relates to my spiritual journey.
## The Chasm of the Pacific Ocean
The Pacific Ocean is massive! It covers [33% ](https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/ocean-fact/pacific-size/)of the earth's surface. When looking straight at the earth with the Pacific Ocean centered, little land is actually visible.  However, remarkably the ancient inhabitants of the Polynesian islands that dot the ocean were able to navigate it using primitive yet very sophisticated methods of what is known as [way-finding.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesian_navigation#cite_note-1) The natural methods included in these way-finding techniques included very subtle observations of the sun, the moon, the stars, fishes of the sea an birds of the air. For example measuring the approximate distance from the horizon to the north star at night one can precisely and accurately calculate how far north they are from the equator. If the Northstar begins to set below the horizon you know you have passed into the southern hemisphere. Even in cloudy conditions, shallow water lagoons from nearby islands can—during the day time—reflect aqua green hues off the bottom of clouds indicating land is nearby. Land-based birds often fly out to sea in the mornings and fly back to land in the evening. Even if there’s no site of land, seeing land base birds is an indicator that you’re not far. As spot lighted in Disney's Moana, the character [Maui says:](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3521164/quotes/?item=qt5136450&ref_=ext_shr_lnk) 

> \[way-finding is\] not just sails and knots. It's seeing where you're going in your mind. Knowing where you are, by knowing where you've been.

## Way-finding as a legend
In Hawaii, traditional legend held that way-finding [went extinct in the 14th century.](https://www.pbs.org/wayfinders/ask.html#:~:text=Over%20the%20last%2015%20years,Polynesia%20in%20the%2014th%20century.) Way-finding as a technique was lost as newer techniques were introduced to larger islands. Leading anthropological research, where studies are conducted to explore human societies and cultures and their developments, argued that the Polynesian Islands were largely populated on accident, as sailing peoples from eastern Asia and western Americas were caught in winds and currents that lead them to the various islands through out.

Nainoa Thompson, a native Hawaiian, studying anthropology at the University of Hawaii become interested in determining whether Way-finding was legitimate or only legend. Through, associations throughout the region, he was able to meet [Pius “Mau” Piailug,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mau_Piailug) who grew up on an island a square-mile in size within the Carolina islands of Micronesia. The size and remote aspects of his home island lended it immunity to outside contact and influence of outside techniques. Way-finding was still practiced in his family: He had learned way-finding from his father and grandfather. According to the Polynesian Voyaging Society, in the 1970's there were [only 6 known individuals who claimed they could "way-find" ](https://the.honoluluadvertiser.com/article/2006/Nov/14/ln/FP611140354.html#:~:text=When%20the%20Polynesian%20Voyaging%20Society%20launched%20Hokule'a%20in%201975%2C%20it%20needed%20a%20navigator.%20But%20the%20last%20Polynesian%20navigator%20had%20died%20in%201969%20and%20only%20six%20deep%2Dsea%20navigators%20were%20known%20to%20exist.)and none of them were willing to share what they knew. Except for Mau.

In 1976, Nainoa, with the the help of Mau's navigational skills, successfully completed a [35-day voyage from Hawaii to Tahiti in a canoe using only traditional methods of way-finding.](https://www.adventure-journal.com/2019/03/mau-piailug-one-of-the-last-wayfinders-followed-the-stars-to-tahiti/?srsltid=AfmBOoqtCoLJTHpSi_3bnQub1q8CcvcwC1c1cnlD6YAUYO0-y1POETmM) Though Mau knew the general direction which he needed to travel, he relied on these cues throughout the voyage to create small course corrections to finally arrive at his final destination. This voyage was the first time in recorded history, outside of legend, that way-finding demonstrated reality. It demonstrated that travel between islands in early Polynesian history was [more likely to be deliberate, rather than accidental.](https://www.adventure-journal.com/2019/03/mau-piailug-one-of-the-last-wayfinders-followed-the-stars-to-tahiti/?srsltid=AfmBOoqAnCKePiNgeGN0FAiMEV_jjPbv-hYm93p5Xxhi812MpegyV7U2#:~:text=The%20voyage%20would%20serve%20as%20living%20proof%20that%20Polynesian%20people%20had%20not%20populated%20the%20Pacific%20by%20accident%2C%20as%20many%20academics%20theorized%20at%20the%20time%2C%20but%20purposefully.) 

# Spiritual Way-finding
For me, I think both developing and nourishing our testimony of Jesus Christ and His Gospel require a sort of spiritual way-finding. As I mentioned earlier, navigating to a point through mortality where we can say we have a testimony can feel challenging. The amount of information we have to today is nuts. Whether the information we have is reliable and trustworthy in some circumstances is becoming harder to ascertain. There have been times I have felt, as Paul in his epistle to the Ephesians put it: easily "[tossed to and fro and carried about with every wind of doctrine by the sleight of men.](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/eph/4?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)" Like Polynesian way-finders using a celestial map to make their course home, God has given us tools to make it back to our celestial home. In Lehi's vision of the tree of life the [iron rod](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p25#p25) functions as an ever present guide, aiding individuals along their path to the tree to partake of and gain first-hand experience of [God's Love.](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p21-p22#p21) Still, the great and spacious building of today is in some ways growing ever spacious. We may feel that our colleagues or peers would love to make us ashamed of the Gospel of Jesus Christ. In last October's General Conference, Kevin G. Brown shared a powerful story, recounting that during his childhood in Jamaica, it felt very painful when many friends, family, and even mentors would ask him: "[How can you believe that book? How can you believe that story!?](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2025/10/24brown?lang=eng&id=p_yAoN3#p_yAoN3)" The midsts of darkness around us may seem to be getting thicker—requiring holders of the rod to trust their grip more than their eyes, and more importantly to the still small voice of the Holy Ghost. As President Nelson is often quoted: "[it will not be possible to survive spiritually without the guiding, directing, comforting, and constant influence of the Holy Ghost.](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2018/04/revelation-for-the-church-revelation-for-our-lives?lang=eng&id=p37#p37)"

# Trial of Faith

## Way-finding
Even in traditional way-finding there is often an additional sense beyond those of explicit observation that develops with experience. Nainoa Thompson who commissioned Mau to sail to Tahiti, originally had no interest in learning way-finding himself. It was only to establish that it was in-fact a verified method of travel. During his voyage with Mau, he found more and more questions arise within him that made him realize he wouldn't really be able to satisfy his understanding of the techniques without experience navigating a voyage himself. He trained with Mau constantly, and then eventually decided to attempt the voyage. [One of the biggest challenges he feared on his journey were the doldrums](https://www.pbs.org/wayfinders/wayfinding3.html). For those that don't know, the doldrums are the weather conditions that sit at the equator, where the sun essentially evaporates the water into storms that are almost constantly there. It is also in a dead zone of jet streams, so any wind that is there moves in chaotic directions. If you take a moment to google "goes west full disk" hit the first link and click on "animation loop" you'll see the most recent live images captured of our planet from satellites. There is always a line of storms that sit over the equator. These are the doldrums. When asked about about his voyaging experience and the doldurms this was his response:

> I just dreaded the doldrums, because I had no confidence that I could get through it. I limited myself to thinking that I could only really accurately navigate with visual celestial clues. Getting into the doldrums, where there's 100 percent cloud cover all of a sudden -- to me, I would be blind. And that's what happened. We got in the doldrums, and it was just a mess. It was 100 percent cloud cover, the wind was switching around, and we were going fast. That's the worst thing you want to do -- moving in a direction and not know where you're going. I was fighting my way to search for anything in this kind of black\[ness\]. It was nighttime, and it was black -- the sky, everything was black -- and I couldn't find anything with my eyes. I  got so exhausted that I backed up against the rail and there was something, some mechanism, that allowed me to understand where the direction was, without seeing it.
> 
> It was almost like, when I gave up fighting to try to find something with my eyes, and just settled down, all of a sudden, it was like this warmth came over me, and I just knew where the moon was. But you couldn't see the moon, it was so black.  I directed the canoe with this total confidence at a time when I had previously convinced myself prior to the voyage that I would have no confidence in knowing where to go. I turned the canoe to this particular direction, got things lined up. Oddly I began to feel very, very comfortable in this cold, wet, rough environment. Heading in this direction led us to a break in the clouds and the moon was there. I don't know how, from a scientific way to make this understandable, you know, it just doesn't make sense -- it's unexplainable, but I know the experience existed.

Nainoa goes on to say:
> And those are the things that I chase now. It's not like I can do that any old time, it's more like special experiences and \[I have\]  to be in the right frame of mind... I can't just conjure up these experiences consciously. But they do come, and they come more and more often now. It has a lot to do with this kind of internal relaxation. I don't know what it is. It just happens. And to tell you the truth, I don't want to analyze it too much. I just want to keep making it happen more often, and let myself naturally obtain it. ...I don't think there's an answer for it, even \[if I were to try and analyze it\].

## Spiritual
The prophet Moroni, in the book of Ether notes: "[for ye receive no witness until after the trial of your faith.](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)" I think it's safe to say that everyone who is serious about gaining a testimony and keeping it fresh will have at least one, if not more, trials of their faith. We all will hit the spiritual doldrums. Even with every tool we have at our disposal: prayer, the scriptures, our peer member and ministers, general conference, prior experience with personal revelation—there inevitably comes a point where we have to pass through the doldrums. Honestly, I think anyone who believes they are immune to this places themself in [dangerous spiritual territory](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2022/04/47nelson?lang=eng&id=p40#p40).

I had mentioned at the beginning of my talk that I felt this growing gap between belief and knowing. This occurred on my mission. All my companions seemed to testify of Moroni's promise with unyielding conviction. While I was certainly willing to testify of my belief in it, I felt a growing personal inferiority that I couldn't testify from personal experience. I recall a point, early in my mission almost begging the Lord nightly for some experience or witness I could draw from with respect to Moroni's promise specifically. I don't think I can say that I wrestled with the Lord in the same way [Enos in the Book of Mormon ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/enos/1?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)did, but I do recalled one particular night struggling a lot with this. It had felt like as the prophet Ezekiel described: "[\[God had covered\] the heavens, and \[made\] the stars thereof dark; \[He covered\]  the sun with a cloud, and the moon \[did\] not give her light.](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ezek/32?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)" I mentally got to a point where I was considering the possibility that the book was either false or of no consequence. However, before I ultimately came to that conclusion, I do remember thinking, at this moment that sort of felt like a brink to me, that "you know what, I want to believe this." With that thought, the anxiety left me. As if the Holy Ghost was telling me to "be still". The impression came to me to focus on what I do know and write down your impressions as you continue, and follow through on what you receive. I decided to write down impressions and do my best to follow through on them. The experiences that followed with investigating friends, companions, and other members marked significant moments that helped me nourish what I did know, and expand upon what I had not yet known; including experience with how the spirit speaks to me. 

One peculiar experience I figure I'll share here—again on my mission—that was both a testament to personal revelation and peculiar ways God manifests his comfort was with my dad and my mission president during my mission. My siblings and I often joke that our dad is, as Lamen and Lemual said of Lehi, "[a visionary man](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)". 2 months before I received my mission call, my dad called me asking me where I thought I was going to be called. He told me he had a dream that I would be called to Budapest Hungary . Two months later I was called there. As I was about to leave the MTC for the mission field, he emailed me, telling me he wasn't sure why he had another dream of my mission but said if its worth anything to know, you'll be going to the east-most city of your mission. That happened. The day I was about to be transferred to my next area, he again emailed me letting me know that I'd be transferred, and that in my next area, he's been having a dream of a farmer who I needed to visit with. In this next city, 1 of our only 2 investigators  was a farmer who lived 20 minutes by bus north of the city  whose friends would become relevant friends to the church. Updates like this from my dad continued to occur in almost transfer to a new location. In my exit interview with my mission president, reflecting on these emails, I asked him: Have you ever had discussions with my father during my mission. He said no. I let him know about the emails I had been receiving. He got a bit emotional, and said thank you for the confirmation. To-date, I'm still unsure why he received these dreams, but perhaps they were a comforting confirmation to my mission president  for the work he was doing with his missionaries.

# Action
For Nainoa in the doldrums seeking which way to go, he found his way by being still and then acting on the impression he received. Similarly, during the period of what felt like spiritual chaos to me, I was able to steer through it by being still and acting on the impression received. In 2 Nephi of the Book of Mormon we read: 

> For behold, thus saith the Lord God: I will give unto the children of men line upon line, precept upon precept, here a little and there a little; and blessed are those who hearken unto my precepts, and lend an ear unto my counsel, for they shall learn wisdom; for unto him that receiveth I will give more

Jesus Christ and His Gospel, His "Good News": His atonement and resurrection, and all doctrine than extends from that is vast. It isn't necessary in mortality to understand it all at once. Simply not having experience with or a witness of any specifically particular piece of doctrine, or aspect of the church doesn't invalidate the res rest of the Church as a whole, just as calculus isn't rendered useless and of no consequence because our understanding of math might only extends to multiplication and division. With any witness we do have, whether it's prayer,  individual passages of scripture, impressions, promptings, personal longitudinal experiences, we have the responsibility to first acknowledge the answers to prayers we have received, and then to act. Like the populating of the Polynesian Islands, developing a testimony cannot happen by accident or luck, it is only through deliberate action on our faith that it can grow.

Referring back to Elder Kevin G. Brown's address from the October general conference, he notes [3 essential truths](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2025/10/24brown?lang=eng&id=p_jDcPw#p_jDcPw) that helped build his testimony. 
1. Knowing that God is our loving heavenly father.
2. Agency is the gift to act for ourselves.
3. Our Testimony comes through the Holy Ghost.

These three key ingredients seem to match exactly what I needed during my mission and what I continue to need today. When the doldrums of the adversary seem to arise and attempt to divert our attention, or swipe us off our feet to loose grip of the iron rod, we can as [Psalms 46: 10 ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/46?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)instructs "Be still and know that I am God." We can trust that as read in [D&C 8:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/8?lang=eng&id=p2#p2) "I will tell you in your mind and in your heart, by the Holy Ghost", and then use our agency to act. If we fail to act, we risk going on the wrong direction. As President Nelson recently[warned](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2022/04/47nelson?lang=eng&id=p40#p40):

> With frightening speed, a testimony that is not nourished daily “by the good word of God” can crumble. Thus, the antidote to Satan’s scheme is clear: we need daily experiences worshipping the Lord and studying His gospel. I plead with you to let God prevail in your life. Give Him a fair share of your time. As you do, notice what happens to your positive spiritual momentum.
### Remembering

As a final comment, Mau was taught the traditional art of way finding by his father and grandfather. Some of these skills and know-how were embedded into song. These songs helped them remember what they were taught. Similarly, sacred music can functions as mental reminders and mnemonics at all times. Sister Tracy Y. Browning of the General Primary Presidency taught:

> "Sacred music can help write the doctrine of Christ into the soul and prepare us to receive His ordinances. It links the Savior’s doctrine to our memory and that memory to our discipleship in Him."

If we find ourselves lost in a seemingly vast expanse of confusion between the small islands of refuge on our journey back to our Father in heaven, whether our view of the stars are obscured: personal experiences seem numbed, dulled, or clouded, we can remember the hymn: "Let the Holy Spirit Guide, let his wisdom govern choice. He will lead you safely home, if we listen to his voice."
